Cubing Beginner Method

I first learnt how to solve a Rubik's cube using the beginner method from JPerm (Dylan Wang), or if you like, Cubehead (Milan Struyf with Matty Inaba) shared a tutorial using the same method recently. I've made a quick reference version of this method below, but if you are just getting started, then you should watch their videos as they explain the steps. This method uses repetitions of the Righty alg (R U R' U') or Lefty alg (L' U' L U) in several stages, with the Sune and Niklas algorithm to orientate the last layer, edges and corners. It is also worth learning some of the faster techniques (e.g. skipping daisy and solving white cross in down layer, or useful finger tricks). With practice, you should find you have the learnt to solve a cube within 2 to 3 minutes. Alternatively, CubeSkills (Felix Zemdegs) teaches a similar method (also copied below) but he uses the Sune, T-perm, and Ua-perm algorithms to solve the last layer of the cube ... I'm going to try this, whilst also learning CFOP (2-look) methods.

Dylan Wang Beginner Method

FIRST LAYER WHITE CROSS

The 1st step is to create a white cross. Hold cube with white face upwards. If white edge is in top or bottom layer, align edge color with its side-face center and rotate that face to bring it into position in the top layer. If edge is in side layer, move it into the bottom and turn it away so you can re-align the edge, then solve as before. If an edge is flipped, you need to rotate the white layer so the edge can be rotated into the target slot.

FIRST LAYER CORNERS

The 2nd step is to solve the first layer. Hold cube with white cross downwards. Rotate top layer so white corner piece is over target slot, between the other colour centers. If the corner has white facing right, use righty alg. If white is facing left use the lefty alg. If white is facing up, use 3 repetitions of the righty alg. If corner is already in the wrong slot, hold it on the right and use righty alg to take it out of the slot, and then re-insert it in as before. Repeat to solve for each corner slot.

The 5th step is to orientate the edges in the third layer. Rotate the upward layer to align as many edges with their center colour (always match 2). If matching edges are next to each other, hold cube so they are back/right, then use Sune algorithm to solve. If they are across from each other, hold them in any pattern, then use Sune algorithm to setup adjacent pair, and repeat for this case. You may need an extra turn to re-align edges with their center.

LAST LAYER CORNER PERMUTATION

The 6th step is to position the corners in the third layer. If 1 corner is in correct slot between its other colour centers, hold it in the front/right. If 0 are correct, hold any corner in the front/right. Use Niklas algorithm to position the corners, and repeat until all four corners are aligned and the edges are over their correct center. You may need an extra turn to re-align edges with their center.

LAST LAYER CORNER ORIENTATION

The 7th step is to solve the corners in the third layer, there by solving the cube. Hold cube flipped, with yellow face downward, with an unsolved corner in front/right position. Repeatedly use the righty alg until the corner is solved. If need to solve other corners, rotate bottom layer (D) to bring the unsolved corner into the front/right position, and repeat the right trigger, do not rotate the cube by mistake.

The 5th step is to orientate the corners of the last layer. Check how many are up in the top layer and hold the cube in the position for that case. Then use the corner orientation (Sune) algorithm, repeated as required for that case (1 corner = 1 or 2 repetitions, 2 corners = 2 or 3 repetitions, 0 corners = 2 or 3 repetitions).

The 6th step is to align the corners of the last layer. Hold two corners on the left side as if headlights, and then execute the (T-perm) algorithm. If there are initially no matching corners, perform the algorithm from any angle, then repeat for headlight case.

LAST LAYER EDGE PERMUTATION

The 7th step is to align the edges of the last layer, which solves the cube. Hold the cube so a solid block, where an edge is solved, is at the back. Then execute the (Ua-perm) algorithm (may need 1 or 2 repetitions depending on case). If there is initially no block, perform the algorithm from any angle.


(R U') (R U) (R U) (R U') R' U' R2
HOLD BLOCK IN BACK

Cubing Notation & Finger Techniques

Holding the cube in a fixed position, there are 6 sides that can be turned. A single letter denotes turning the specified face 90 degrees clockwise (e.g. F). A letter followed by a prime symbol (e.g. R') denotes a 90 degree turn anti-clockwise. A letter followed by a 2 denotes a double turn of that face (e.g. U2).

Most cubing algorithms are dominated by up-face (U), right-face (R) and front-face (F) rotations. Some case will use left-face (e.g. L or L') turns, and some occasional cases use down-face (D) or back-face (B) turns, or cube rotations (e.g. y) or slice moves (e.g. M).

Orientation refers to ensuring all the pieces are turned correctly within their spots (e.g. entire top layer the same colour). Permutation refers to moving these oriented pieces around so they are in their correct spot (e.g. surrounded and aligned with each matching colour face).
