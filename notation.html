Cubing Notation & Finger Techniques

Explanation

Holding the cube in a fixed position, there are 6 sides that can be turned. An uppercase letter (e.g. F) denotes turning the specified face 90 degrees clockwise as if looking at that face. A letter followed by a prime symbol (e.g. R') denotes a 90 degree turn anti-clockwise. A letter followed by 2 (e.g. U2) denotes a 180 degree turn of that face. A lowercase letter, means a wide rotation (face + middle layer) or a cube rotation.

Most cubing algorithms are dominated by up-face (U), right-face (R) and front-face (F) turns. Some cases will use left-face (L) turns, and less-so, some cases use down-face (D) or back-face (B) turns, or cube rotations or slice moves. Keep in mind, all scrambles start with cube orientated with white as up-face, green as front-face.

Orientation refers to ensuring all the pieces are turned correctly within their spots (e.g. entire top layer the same colour). Permutation refers to moving these oriented pieces around so they are in their correct spot (e.g. surrounded and aligned with each matching colour face).

Logical groups of moves are denoted by round bracket, some of which are named triggers, for example: sexy (R U R' U'), extract pair (R U R'), insert pair (R U' R'), reverse sexy (U R U' R'), and sledgehammer (R' F R F').
