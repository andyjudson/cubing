Cubing Notation & Finger Techniques

Notation Explained

A rubik's cube has 6 coloured faces or sides that can be turned, each comprised of 1 fixed center piece + 4 edge pieces (2 colours) + 4 corner pieces (3 colours). Scrambles are generated with the cube orientated so white is up-face, green is front-face.

An uppercase letter (e.g. F) denotes a 90 degree clockwise turn of that face, as if looking towards it. A letter followed by a prime symbol (e.g. R') denotes a 90 degree anticlockwise turn. A letter followed by 2 (e.g. U2) denotes a 180 degree turn of that face. A lowercase letter, means a wide turn based on that face + middle layer (e.g. r), or a cube rotation on that axis (e.g. y).

Most cubing algorithms are dominated by up-face (U), right-face (R), left-face (L) and front-face (F) turns. Less so, some algorithms will use down-face (D), or back-face (B) turns, or cube rotations, or slice moves.

Orientation means moving pieces so that the face of the layer is all the same colour. Permutation means moving these oriented pieces around so that they are also matching the colour of their other faces.

Logical groups of moves are denoted by round bracket so easier to remember, some of which are named triggers, for example: sexy (R U R' U'), extract pair (R U R'), insert pair (R U' R'), reverse sexy (U R U' R'), and sledgehammer (R' F R F').
